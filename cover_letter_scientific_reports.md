Dear Editors,

Please consider my manuscript, "Pairwise, Style-Controlled, Nonparametric Tests for Judge Bias under the ISU Judging System," for publication in *Scientific Reports*. I am the sole author, Michael Allman (Independent Researcher; University of Chicago Booth School of Business, MBA). The study introduces and applies a residual-label permutation framework to test for systematic judge bias while controlling for performance "style" via nonparametric, pairwise comparisons under the ISU judging system.

Empirically, the method is applied to 142 events across 17 ISU competitions (2022–2026), comprising 291,604 individual judge marks. At a Benjamini–Hochberg false discovery rate threshold of q ≤ 0.05, the analysis identifies 20,213 significant judge × competitor-pair results, and further isolates 9 outcome-determinative events in which the detected effects are large enough to change the gold-medal winner.

A flagship result is reported for the 2026 Olympic Winter Games Ice Dance Free Dance, where one judge (J1) exhibits a statistically significant positive shift of +1.19 points for a competitor relative to a close comparator (p = 0.0003, q = 0.012), exceeding the 0.97-point gold–silver margin. This example illustrates how the proposed approach can quantify both statistical and competitive significance within real ISU event structures.

All data and materials are openly available in accordance with *Scientific Reports* policies: the dataset is deposited on Zenodo (https://doi.org/10.5281/zenodo.18782656) and the full analysis code is available on GitHub (https://github.com/michaelallman1960-max/figure-skating-judge-bias). The manuscript is not under review elsewhere.
